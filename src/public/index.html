About Quantum

According to Wikipedia In physics, a quantum (plural: quanta) is the minimum number of each physical entity (physical property) involved in an interaction. The fundamental idea that physical properties can be "quantized" is called the "quantization hypothesis".[1] This means that the magnitude of a physical property can only take discrete values consisting of integer multiples of a single quantum.

Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is a fundamental branch of physics used to explain atomic and subatomic systems. The concept of quantum mechanics is used to replace classical mechanics. Quantum mechanics is in quantum superposition so it is incompatible with classical physics. Quantum mechanics is used to establish a mathematical frame of reference for atomic physics, molecular physics, computational chemistry, quantum chemistry, particle physics, and nuclear physics.
